Privacy Policy

Commitments  ·  Last updated: June 11, 2026

Commitments is a personal deadline and task-scheduling tool. This policy explains what data the app collects, where it is stored, how your Google Calendar data is used, and what rights you have over your information.

Short version: Your task data is stored in a private database tied to your account. Your Google Calendar data is used only to calculate your availability and write work blocks you explicitly request — it is never stored on our servers, shared, or used for any other purpose.

1. Who operates this app

Commitments is a personal project operated by an individual developer. It is not a commercial product. If you have questions or requests about your data, contact: github.com/jbrownsberger.

2. Account & authentication data

Sign-in is handled by Supabase Auth (hosted on Supabase’s infrastructure in the us-east-1 region). When you create an account the following is stored:

  • Your email address (used only for authentication)
  • A hashed password (Supabase never stores passwords in plain text)
  • A randomly generated user UUID
  • Account creation and last sign-in timestamps

Supabase’s own privacy policy applies to this data: supabase.com/privacy.

3. Task & scheduling data

All task data you create is stored in a private PostgreSQL database hosted on Supabase. Row-Level Security (RLS) is enforced on every table, meaning each row is accessible only by the authenticated user who created it. The data stored includes:

  • Tasks — name, status, priority, due date, estimated hours, progress, notes, recurrence settings, and per-day scheduled hours
  • Substeps — checklist items belonging to a task (text, done state)
  • Quick tasks — lightweight time-boxed to-dos with optional deadlines
  • Categories — user-defined labels and colours for grouping tasks
  • Scheduled days — the calendar dates on which tasks are planned
  • User preferences — weekly hour target, default session length, dark-mode preference

This data is used solely to operate the app for you. It is never sold, shared with third parties, or used for advertising.

4. Google Calendar integration

The Google Calendar feature is entirely optional. If you choose to connect, the app requests the following OAuth scope:

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/calendar — read/write access to your Google Calendar

This scope is used for three specific purposes:

  • Reading availability — the app queries Google’s free/busy API to calculate how many free hours you have each day. Only busy/free time intervals are read; event titles, descriptions, attendees, and other event details are never accessed or stored.
  • Writing work blocks — when you click “Add to GCal”, the app creates a calendar event in the calendar you select. The event contains only the task name, a scheduled time slot, and an internal tag so the app can identify its own events.
  • Deleting work blocks — when you remove a scheduled block, the app deletes the corresponding event it previously created.
Google Calendar data is processed transiently in your browser only. No calendar event content, titles, or attendee information is ever sent to or stored on Commitments’ servers.

Your Google OAuth access token is cached in your browser’s localStorage for the duration of your session so you do not have to re-authenticate on every page load. It is never transmitted to the app’s backend. You can revoke access at any time at myaccount.google.com/permissions.

The app’s use of Google Calendar data complies with the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

5. Local storage

The following settings are stored only in your browser’s localStorage and never sent to any server:

  • Google Calendar settings (working windows, buffer time, efficiency, non-work days)
  • Selected calendar IDs for availability calculation
  • The ID of your chosen work-blocks calendar
  • Your Google OAuth access token (session only)

You can clear all of this at any time by disconnecting Google Calendar in the app or clearing your browser’s site data.

6. Cookies & tracking

Commitments does not use cookies, analytics services, advertising trackers, or any third-party tracking scripts. No data about your usage is shared with advertising platforms.

7. Data retention & deletion

Your account and all associated task data remain stored until you request deletion. To delete your account and all data, contact the developer via GitHub. All records tied to your user ID across every database table will be permanently removed within 30 days of your request.

8. Data security

All data in transit is encrypted via TLS. Data at rest is encrypted by Supabase. Row-Level Security policies on the database ensure no user can read or modify another user’s data, even in the event of an application bug.

9. Children’s privacy

Commitments is not directed at children under 13. The app does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 13 years of age.

10. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, the “Last updated” date at the top will be revised. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
